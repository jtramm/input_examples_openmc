KANT Beryllium Spherical Shell Benchmark
=========================================

Source:  SINBAD database (Shielding Integral Benchmark Archive and Database)
         KANT experiment at Forschungszentrum Karlsruhe (FZK), Germany

URL:     https://www.oecd-nea.org/science/wprs/shielding/sinbad/kant/fzk-be_a.htm

Description:
  The Karlsruhe Neutron Transmission (KANT) experiment measured neutron
  leakage spectra through concentric spherical beryllium shells with a
  central T(d,n) 14.1 MeV neutron source. Three shell thicknesses were
  tested: 5 cm, 10 cm, and 17 cm, all with an inner radius of 5 cm.

  The key physics feature is the Be-9(n,2n) neutron multiplication reaction,
  which has a threshold of approximately 1.85 MeV. Because the 14.1 MeV
  source neutrons are well above this threshold, the total neutron leakage
  exceeds the source strength -- more neutrons emerge from the shell than
  enter it. This neutron multiplication property makes beryllium essential
  in fusion reactor blanket designs for tritium breeding.

Measurements:
  - 178-group neutron leakage spectra from 50 keV to 15 MeV
  - Five-group partial leakage multiplications
  - Detectors: NE-213 scintillator (> 3 MeV), proton recoil proportional
    counters (50 keV - 6 MeV), time-of-flight (< 100 keV), Bonner spheres
  - Measurement angle: 60 degrees from deuteron beam axis
  - Combined uncertainty: 8% (1-sigma)

Reference:
  SINBAD Abstract NEA-1553/73 (FZK-BE)
