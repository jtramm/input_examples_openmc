FNG-ITER Streaming Experiment: Neutron Streaming Through ITER-Like Duct
=========================================================================

Source:      SINBAD (Shielding Integral Benchmark Archive and Database)
             OECD Nuclear Energy Agency
Facility:    Frascati Neutron Generator (FNG), ENEA Frascati, Italy

URL:
  https://www.oecd-nea.org/science/wprs/shielding/sinbad/fng_str/fngstr-a.htm

Description:
  A 14 MeV D-T neutron streaming experiment through an ITER-like shielding
  assembly containing a narrow cylindrical duct (28 mm inner diameter,
  39.07 cm long), a rectangular detector cavity (148 x 48 x 52 mm), and
  rear Cu/SS316 plates simulating a superconducting magnet coil.

  The assembly is 100 x 100 cm in cross-section and 94.26 cm deep, composed
  of alternating layers of perspex (PMMA), stainless steel AISI-316, copper,
  and polyethylene. The streaming channel penetrates the front perspex/SS316
  region, simulating a diagnostic or instrumentation port through the ITER
  blanket/shield.

  Two source configurations were measured:
    - On-axis:  D-T source centred on the channel axis
    - Off-axis: D-T source shifted 5.3 cm laterally

  This is the most complex fusion shielding benchmark from the FNG facility
  and exercises neutron streaming, deep penetration, and variance reduction
  capabilities of Monte Carlo transport codes.

Geometry:
  - Assembly: 100 x 100 x 94.26 cm (width x height x depth)
  - Front Cu plate: 1 cm (first wall simulator)
  - Perspex/SS316 layers: alternating blocks with 3 mm SS316 plates
  - Streaming channel: 28 mm ID, 1 mm SS316 wall, 39.07 cm long
  - Detector cavity: 148 x 48 x 52 mm (x, y, z), centred on axis
  - Cu/SS316 coil simulator: ~16.8 cm thick
  - Polyethylene rear shield: ~31 cm
  - D-T point source at 5.3 cm from front face

Measurements:
  - Activation foil reaction rates at 23 positions along the axis:
    4 in channel, 11 in cavity, 8 behind cavity
  - Reactions: Nb-93(n,2n), Al-27(n,alpha), Ni-58(n,p), Au-197(n,gamma)
  - Nuclear heating with CaF2:Tm TLD-300 detectors
