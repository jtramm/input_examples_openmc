FNG Copper Benchmark: Slab Assembly Activation Measurements
=============================================================

Source:      SINBAD (Shielding Integral Benchmark Archive and Database)
             OECD Nuclear Energy Agency
Facility:    Frascati Neutron Generator (FNG), ENEA Frascati, Italy

URL:
  https://www.oecd-nea.org/science/wprs/shielding/sinbad/

Description:
  A rectangular assembly of seven OFHC (Oxygen-Free High-Conductivity)
  copper plates, each approximately 10 cm thick and 60 x 60 cm in cross-
  section, was irradiated by 14 MeV D-T neutrons at the FNG facility.
  Activation foils of seven different types were placed between the plates
  at seven depth positions to measure neutron reaction rate profiles through
  the copper assembly.

  The experiment is part of a series of benchmark experiments performed at
  FNG for validating nuclear data and transport codes relevant to fusion
  reactor shielding and activation calculations. Copper is a critical
  material for ITER first-wall heat sinks, divertor cooling structures,
  and RF antenna components.

Geometry:
  - Rectangular assembly: 60 x 60 x ~70 cm (7 plates x 10 cm each)
  - OFHC copper plates stacked along beam axis
  - D-T point source at 5.3 cm from front face

Measurements:
  - Activation foil reaction rates at 7 depth positions:
    Nb-93(n,2n), Al-27(n,alpha), Ni-58(n,p), In-115(n,n'),
    Au-197(n,2n), W-186(n,gamma), Au-197(n,gamma)
  - Reaction rate profiles vs depth for each foil type
  - C/E ratios computed against MCNP reference calculations

Reference calculations:
  - MCNP with FENDL and ENDF/B libraries
  - Validated with OpenMC in published studies

Key references:
  M. Angelone, M. Pillon, et al., "Neutron Activation Measurements in
  Copper for the Validation of Nuclear Data for Fusion Applications,"
  Fusion Eng. Des., 2002.

  P. Batistoni et al., "Analysis of Copper Benchmark Experiment at FNG
  with the European Activation System (EASY)," Fusion Eng. Des., 2001.

  SINBAD Compilation, "FNG Copper Benchmark Experiment,"
  NEA/NSC/DOC, OECD Nuclear Energy Agency.
