Simplified ITER Tokamak 40-Degree Sector Benchmark
====================================================

Source:      ITER Organization design documents and published neutronics analyses
Facility:    ITER, CEA Cadarache, Saint-Paul-les-Durance, France

Description:
  Simplified CSG model of one 40-degree sector (1/9 of the torus, corresponding
  to 2 toroidal field coil periods) of the ITER tokamak. The geometry consists
  of concentric toroidal shells with circular cross-sections centred on a major
  radius of 620 cm. The model includes all major radial build components from
  the plasma to the TF coil outer case.

  This is a simplified benchmark intended for code validation and educational
  purposes. The actual ITER neutronics models use detailed CAD-based geometry
  with elongated D-shaped plasma cross-sections, port penetrations, and
  component-level detail.

Device parameters:
  - Major radius R0:           620 cm
  - Plasma minor radius a:     200 cm (circular cross-section)
  - Sector angle:              40 degrees (18 TF coils -> 360/9 = 40 deg)
  - D-T fusion power:          500 MW (400 MW in neutrons at 14.1 MeV)
  - Magnetic field on axis:    5.3 T (not modelled)

Radial build (minor radius from magnetic axis):
  Component                      Inner (cm)  Outer (cm)  Thickness (cm)
  ----------------------------   ----------  ----------  --------------
  Plasma (void)                       0         200          200
  Beryllium armor                   200         201            1
  Copper heat sink                  201         202            1
  SS316LN first wall structure      202         204            2
  Shielding blanket (SS+H2O)       204         249           45
  Blanket-VV gap (void)            249         253            4
  VV inner shell (SS316LN)         253         259            6
  VV fill (SS316LN+H2O)           259         289           30
  VV outer shell (SS316LN)        289         295            6
  VV-TS gap (void)                295         300            5
  Thermal shield (SS304)          300         302            2
  TS-TF gap (void)                302         310            8
  TF coil inner case (SS316LN)   310         315            5
  TF winding pack (homogenized)  315         340           25
  TF coil outer case (SS316LN)   340         345            5

Materials:
  - Beryllium armor: 1.85 g/cm^3, natural Be
  - Copper heat sink: 8.96 g/cm^3, natural Cu (69.17% Cu-63, 30.83% Cu-65)
  - SS316LN: 7.93 g/cm^3 (Fe 62.5%, Cr 17.5%, Ni 12.5%, Mo 2.5%,
    Mn 2%, Si 1%, N 0.15%, C 0.03% by weight)
  - Blanket/VV fill: homogenized SS316LN + H2O, 60/40 by volume,
    effective density 5.158 g/cm^3
  - TF winding pack: homogenized SS316LN (50%) + Cu (30%) + Nb (15%) +
    Sn (5%) by volume, effective density ~8.3 g/cm^3

Source:
  - D-T fusion neutrons at 14.1 MeV, isotropic emission
  - Uniformly distributed in plasma volume (R = 460-780 cm, Z = -160
    to +160 cm, phi = 0 to 40 degrees)

Reference values (from ITER design analyses):
  - Peak neutron wall loading (outboard midplane): ~1.0 MW/m^2
  - Average neutron wall loading: ~0.56 MW/m^2
  - Nuclear heating in blanket: ~85% of neutron power (~340 MW)
  - TF coil nuclear heating: < 10-20 kW (design limit for cryogenics)
  - Fast neutron fluence at TF coil (E > 0.1 MeV): < 10^22 n/m^2 lifetime
  - Shielding attenuation factor: ~10^10 (plasma to TF coil)
