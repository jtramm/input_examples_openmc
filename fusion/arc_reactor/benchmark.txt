ARC Reactor Fusion Neutronics Benchmark
========================================

Design:     ARC (Affordable, Robust, Compact) tokamak
Origin:     MIT Plasma Science and Fusion Center (PSFC)
Type:       Compact high-field D-T fusion pilot plant concept

URL:
  https://doi.org/10.1016/j.fusengdes.2015.07.008  (Sorbom et al. 2015)
  https://doi.org/10.1016/j.fusengdes.2018.09.007  (Kuang et al. 2018)

Description:
  The ARC reactor is a compact tokamak concept that exploits
  high-temperature superconducting (HTS) REBCO magnets to achieve a
  toroidal field of 9.2 T on axis in a device with major radius
  R = 3.3 m and minor radius a = 1.13 m. The design produces ~525 MW
  of D-T fusion power.

  The blanket uses molten FLiBe (Li2BeF4) as a combined tritium breeder,
  neutron moderator, and primary coolant. The liquid salt fills an ~80 cm
  thick annular region surrounding the plasma chamber. Tritium is bred
  via Li-6(n,t)He-4 and Li-7(n,n't)He-4 reactions, while Be-9 provides
  neutron multiplication via (n,2n).

  The neutronics model is a 20-degree toroidal sector (1/18 of the full
  torus, corresponding to one TF coil period) with reflective boundary
  conditions. The cross-section is built from concentric ZTorus surfaces
  with circular profiles.

Geometry (radial build from plasma axis outward):
  - Plasma:        void, minor radius 113 cm
  - First wall:    Inconel 718, 3 cm thick (113 - 116 cm)
  - FLiBe blanket: Li2BeF4 molten salt, 80 cm thick (116 - 196 cm)
  - Vacuum vessel: Inconel 718, 10 cm thick (196 - 206 cm)
  - Neutron shield: Borated steel (2 wt% B), 10 cm thick (206 - 216 cm)
  - TF coil:       Cu/SS/HTS composite, 30 cm thick (216 - 246 cm)

Device parameters:
  - Major radius:      R = 3.3 m
  - Minor radius:      a = 1.13 m
  - Elongation:        kappa = 1.84
  - Triangularity:     delta = 0.52
  - Toroidal field:    B_T = 9.2 T (on axis)
  - Fusion power:      ~525 MW
  - TF coils:          18

Key neutronics quantities:
  - Tritium Breeding Ratio (TBR): ~1.05 - 1.10 with natural Li
    (TBR ~1.30 with 90% enriched Li-6)
  - Three independent Monte Carlo codes agreed within 0.6%

Materials:
  - FLiBe (Li2BeF4): density 1.94 g/cm^3, natural Li (7.5% Li-6)
  - Inconel 718: density 8.19 g/cm^3
  - Borated steel: SS316-type + 2 wt% natural boron, density 7.65 g/cm^3
  - TF coil composite: 40% Cu / 50% SS / 10% Ni (Hastelloy), density 8.0 g/cm^3

Source:
  - D-T fusion neutrons at 14.1 MeV
  - Distributed uniformly in cylindrical annulus: R in [300, 360] cm,
    Z in [-50, 50] cm, phi in [0, 20 deg]
  - Isotropic angular distribution

Cross-section data:
  - ENDF/B-VIII.0
