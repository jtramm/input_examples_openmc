CFETR 22.5-Degree Toroidal Sector (WCCB Blanket)
==================================================

Source:      Conceptual design studies for the China Fusion Engineering
             Test Reactor (CFETR)
Facility:    CFETR (proposed), Institute of Plasma Physics, Chinese
             Academy of Sciences (ASIPP), Hefei, China

Description:
  A simplified 22.5-degree toroidal sector (1/16 of full torus, one TF coil
  period) of the CFETR tokamak with the Water-Cooled Ceramic Breeder (WCCB)
  blanket concept. CFETR is designed to bridge the gap between ITER and a
  demonstration fusion power plant, targeting steady-state operation, tritium
  self-sufficiency, and electricity generation.

  The WCCB blanket uses:
    - Li4SiO4 ceramic pebbles (60% Li-6 enrichment) as tritium breeder
    - Beryllium pebbles as neutron multiplier
    - CLF-1 RAFM steel as structural material
    - Pressurized water as coolant

  This benchmark validates:
    - Tritium Breeding Ratio (TBR) calculations for the WCCB concept
    - Nuclear heating distribution across tokamak components
    - Neutron multiplication efficiency (Be-9(n,2n) reaction)
    - Fast neutron flux attenuation through the radial build
    - Shielding performance of the blanket + VV system

Device parameters:
  - Major radius: 560 cm (5.6 m)
  - Plasma minor radius: 130 cm (1.3 m)
  - Elongation: 1.8 (circular cross-section used in this simplified model)
  - Fusion power: 200 MW (Phase I) to 1000 MW (Phase II)
  - Number of TF coils: 16
  - Plasma current: 10 MA

Radial build (from plasma axis outward):
  - Plasma chamber: 0 - 130 cm (void)
  - First wall: 130 - 132 cm (CLF-1 RAFM steel, 2 cm)
  - Breeding blanket: 132 - 182 cm (homogenized WCCB, 50 cm)
  - Back support structure: 182 - 197 cm (CLF-1 + H2O 70/30, 15 cm)
  - VV inner wall: 197 - 201 cm (SS316LN, 4 cm)
  - VV fill: 201 - 221 cm (SS316 + H2O 60/40, 20 cm)
  - VV outer wall: 221 - 225 cm (SS316LN, 4 cm)
  - Thermal shield: 225 - 227 cm (SS304, 2 cm)
  - TF coil: 227 - 252 cm (SS316 + Cu, 25 cm)

Expected results (from literature):
  - Global TBR: ~1.1-1.3 with 60% Li-6 enrichment and full blanket coverage
    (achieving TBR > 1.2 is noted as a design challenge by Zhu et al.)
  - Nuclear heating dominated by blanket region (~80% of total)
  - Be(n,2n) multiplication essential for TBR > 1.0
  - Fast flux at VV attenuated by ~3-4 orders of magnitude relative to FW
