ST-FNSF Benchmark: Spherical Tokamak Fusion Nuclear Science Facility
======================================================================

Source:      Princeton Plasma Physics Laboratory (PPPL)
             J. E. Menard et al., Nuclear Fusion, 2016

URL:
  https://doi.org/10.1088/0029-5515/56/10/106023

Description:
  The Spherical Tokamak Fusion Nuclear Science Facility (ST-FNSF) is a
  compact fusion device concept designed as a stepping stone between
  present-day experiments and a demonstration power plant (DEMO). It
  operates at very low aspect ratio (A = 1.7), producing a compact
  device with a major radius of only 1.7 m.

  The ST-FNSF concept is designed to produce 60-100 MW of fusion power
  with a neutron wall loading of approximately 1.0 MW/m^2. It serves as
  a facility to test tritium breeding blanket modules, qualify materials
  under fusion neutron irradiation, and validate nuclear data and codes.

  The key neutronics challenge of the spherical tokamak is the extremely
  compact center column. The distance from the tokamak axis to the
  plasma inner edge is only 70 cm, leaving very limited space for
  shielding. There is no room for a breeding blanket on the inboard
  side, so all tritium breeding must occur on the outboard.

Device Parameters:
  Major radius:      R0 = 1.7 m (170 cm)
  Minor radius:      a  = 1.0 m (100 cm)
  Aspect ratio:      A  = 1.7
  Elongation:        kappa = 2.8
  Fusion power:      60-100 MW (FNSF phase)
  Neutron wall load: ~1.0 MW/m^2
  TF coils:          12 (30-degree sector symmetry)

Geometry:
  This OpenMC model uses a simplified CSG representation of one
  30-degree toroidal sector with reflective boundary conditions.

  Inboard (R < 170 cm):
    - Copper center post:    R = 0 to 50 cm
    - WC+water shield:       R = 50 to 70 cm
    - Plasma (void):         R = 70 to 170 cm

  Outboard (R > 170 cm):
    - Plasma (void):         R = 170 to 270 cm
    - F82H first wall:       3 cm thick
    - DCLL blanket:          60 cm thick (LiPb + RAFM + He)
    - WC+water shield:       30 cm thick
    - SS316 vacuum vessel:   18 cm thick

Materials:
  - Copper (8.96 g/cm3): center post TF coil conductor
  - WC + 20% water (12.7 g/cm3): neutron shield
  - F82H RAFM steel (7.89 g/cm3): first wall structure
  - DCLL homogenized (7.1 g/cm3): 25% RAFM + 65% LiPb + 10% He
  - Pb-17Li with 90% Li-6 enrichment (9.4 g/cm3): breeder
  - SS316 (7.93 g/cm3): vacuum vessel

Measured/Expected Quantities:
  - Tritium Breeding Ratio (TBR): ~1.0 (marginal, no inboard blanket)
  - Nuclear heating in all components
  - Center column neutron flux and spectrum
  - Fast flux at TF coil region
  - Neutron wall loading asymmetry (inboard vs outboard)
