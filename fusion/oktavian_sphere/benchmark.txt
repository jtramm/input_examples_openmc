OKTAVIAN Iron Sphere Benchmark
==============================

Source:  SINBAD (Shielding Integral Benchmark Archive Database)
         OECD Nuclear Energy Agency

URL:     https://www.oecd-nea.org/science/wprs/shielding/sinbad/oktav_fe/okfe-abs.htm

Facility
--------
The OKTAVIAN (Osaka-university Toolkit for Verification and Analysis of
Neutronics) facility at Osaka University, Japan.  A series of sphere
transmission experiments were performed with various materials including
iron, aluminium, silicon, nitrogen, oxygen, and others.

Experiment
----------
A 14.1 MeV deuterium-tritium (D-T) neutron source is placed at the center
of a sphere of carbon steel (iron).  The neutron leakage spectrum is measured
by time-of-flight (TOF) at a detector approximately 9.5 m from the source.

Key Parameters
--------------
  Material        : Carbon steel (JIS SS41), ~98.69 wt% Fe
  Sphere radius   : 50.32 cm
  Density         : 7.874 g/cm^3
  Source energy    : 14.1 MeV (monoenergetic, isotropic)
  Measurement     : Neutron leakage energy spectrum (~10 keV to 15 MeV)

Purpose
-------
This benchmark tests the accuracy of iron cross sections for fusion
neutronics applications.  It is sensitive to elastic and inelastic
scattering, the (n,2n) reaction, and resonance structure in iron.
