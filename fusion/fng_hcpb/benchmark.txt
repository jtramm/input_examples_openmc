FNG HCPB Tritium Breeder Module Mock-up
=========================================

Source:      SINBAD (Shielding Integral Benchmark Archive and Database)
             OECD Nuclear Energy Agency
Facility:    Frascati Neutron Generator (FNG), ENEA Frascati, Italy

URL:
  https://www.oecd-nea.org/science/wprs/shielding/sinbad/fng_hcpb/fnghcpb-a.htm

Description:
  A mock-up of the Helium-Cooled Pebble Bed (HCPB) breeding blanket concept,
  one of the two European candidate Test Blanket Modules (TBMs) for ITER. The
  assembly consists of a metallic beryllium neutron multiplier with two double-
  layer breeder sections of Li2CO3 powder enclosed in an AISI-303 stainless
  steel box, plus a rear AISI-316 stainless steel cassette filled with Li2CO3
  powder.

  This is a KEY benchmark for validating tritium breeding calculations in
  fusion blanket designs. The fundamental physics tested are:
    - Be-9(n,2n) neutron multiplication
    - Li-6(n,t) tritium breeding
    - Neutron moderation in beryllium
    - Parasitic absorption in structural steel

Geometry:
  - Main box (AISI-303 SS): 31.0 x 29.0 x 30.9 cm, 0.5 cm walls
  - Rear cassette (AISI-316 SS): 31.0 x 14.8 x 30.9 cm, 0.5 cm walls
  - Beryllium metal fill (density 1.85 g/cm3)
  - Two double-layer breeder sections:
      Each: 1.2 cm Li2CO3 + 1 mm SS divider + 1.2 cm Li2CO3
  - Rear cassette filled with Li2CO3 powder (density 0.9413 g/cm3)
  - D-T point source at 5.3 cm from front face

Measurements:
  - Tritium production rates (Li-6(n,t) and Li-7(n,t)) at 16 positions
  - Activation foil reaction rates (Au, Ni, Al, Nb) at 4 depths:
    y = 4.2, 10.5, 16.8, 23.1 cm from front surface
  - TLD-300 for nuclear heating in breeder layers
  - Total tritium production per breeder section

Reference calculations:
  - MCNP-4C with FENDL-2.1 and JEFF-3.1 nuclear data libraries
  - DORT/TORT deterministic transport calculations for comparison

Key references:
  P. Batistoni et al., "Neutronics experiment on a mock-up of the Helium-

  at the Frascati Neutron Generator," Nucl. Fusion 41 (2001) 1535.
