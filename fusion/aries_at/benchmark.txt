ARIES-AT Advanced Tokamak Fusion Power Plant
===============================================

Source:      ARIES Project (Advanced Reactor Innovation and Evaluation Studies)
             University of California San Diego, in collaboration with national
             laboratories and universities under US DOE support

Description:
  ARIES-AT (Advanced Tokamak) is a conceptual design study for an advanced
  commercial fusion power plant. It represents the most optimistic end of the
  tokamak design space, assuming advanced physics (high beta, high bootstrap
  current fraction) and advanced technology (SiC/SiC composites, high-
  temperature Brayton cycle). The design achieves 1755 MW fusion power in a
  compact device (R = 5.2 m, a = 1.3 m) with a cost of electricity competitive
  with other advanced energy sources.

  The blanket concept uses SiC composite as the structural material and
  Pb-17Li (lead-lithium eutectic) as both the tritium breeder and primary
  coolant. Lithium is enriched to 90% Li-6 for high tritium breeding ratio.
  Neutron multiplication is provided by the Pb(n,2n) reaction in the lead
  component of the Pb-17Li alloy.

  This model implements a simplified CSG toroidal sector for neutronics
  scoping calculations.

Device Parameters:
  - Major radius: 5.2 m (520 cm)
  - Minor radius: 1.3 m (130 cm)
  - Elongation: 2.2
  - Fusion power: 1755 MW
  - Neutron wall loading: ~3.2 MW/m2 (outboard midplane)
  - Bootstrap current fraction: 91%
  - Plasma beta: 9.2%
  - TF coils: 16
  - Net electric power: ~1000 MWe
  - Thermal efficiency: ~59% (Brayton cycle)

Radial Build:
  - First wall: SiC composite, 0.4 cm
  - Blanket: SiC/LiPb (40% SiC, 55% LiPb, 5% void), 30 cm
  - Back wall/manifold: SiC/LiPb, 10 cm
  - Shield: WC/H2O (80/20 vol%), 25 cm
  - Vacuum vessel: F82H ferritic steel, 15 cm
  - TF coil: Nb3Sn/Cu/SS316, 30 cm

Reference Results (neutronics):
  - TBR with 90% Li-6 enrichment: ~1.1
  - Energy multiplication factor: ~1.15
  - Blanket captures ~70% of fusion energy
  - Peak neutron wall loading: ~3.2 MW/m2
