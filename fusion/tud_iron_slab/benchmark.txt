TUD Iron Slab Benchmark
========================

Source:      SINBAD (Shielding Integral Benchmark Archive and Database)
Entry:       TUD Iron Slab Experiment
Facility:    Technische Universitaet Dresden (TUD), Germany

URL:
  https://www.oecd-nea.org/science/wprs/shielding/sinbad/tud_fe/tufe-abs.htm

Description:
  Spectral neutron and photon flux measurements were performed behind large
  iron slab assemblies (100 x 100 cm front face, 30 cm thick) irradiated by
  14 MeV D-T neutrons at TUD. Three configurations were tested:

    A0: Solid slab (no gap) -- baseline deep-penetration measurement
    A1: 5 cm vertical gap at 10 cm from centre -- streaming configuration
    A2: 5 cm vertical gap at 20 cm from centre -- streaming configuration

  The slab was constructed from 20 x 10 x 5 cm iron blocks. An NE213
  scintillation detector measured neutron (above 1 MeV) and photon (above
  0.2 MeV) spectra simultaneously at 300 cm behind the slab rear face.
  Supplementary proportional counters and a stilbene scintillator covered
  the 30 keV to 2.3 MeV neutron energy range. Time-of-arrival spectra were
  also recorded.

  The gap configurations (A1, A2) are particularly relevant for studying
  radiation streaming through penetrations in fusion reactor shields, such
  as beam ports, diagnostic channels, and coolant pipes.

Geometry:
  - Iron slab: 100 x 100 cm frontal area, 30 cm thick
  - Built from 20 x 10 x 5 cm iron blocks
  - Source-to-slab distance: 19 cm
  - Slab-to-detector distance: 300 cm
  - Total source-to-detector: 349 cm
  - D-beam angle: 74 degrees relative to source-slab axis

Material:
  - Natural iron (Fe), density ~7.874 g/cm3

Measurements:
  - Neutron energy spectra (NE213: > 1 MeV; proportional counters: 30 keV - 2.3 MeV)
  - Photon energy spectra (NE213: > 0.2 MeV)
  - Time-of-arrival spectra
  - Three slab configurations (A0, A1, A2)

Reference calculations:
  - MCNP-4A with FENDL-1 and EFF-2 nuclear data libraries

Key references:
  H. Freiesleben, W. Hansen, D.Onal, K. Seidel, S. Unholzer,
  "Neutron and Photon Flux Spectra Measurements for an Iron Slab Assembly
  Bombarded with 14.8 MeV Neutrons," TU Dresden reports.

  SINBAD database compilation, NEA/OECD.
