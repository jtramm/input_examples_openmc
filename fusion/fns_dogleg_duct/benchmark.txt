FNS Dogleg Duct Streaming Experiment
======================================

Source:      SINBAD (Shielding Integral Benchmark Archive and Database)
Entry:       FNS Dogleg Duct Streaming Experiment
Facility:    Fusion Neutronics Source (FNS), JAEA, Tokai-mura, Japan

URL:
  https://www.oecd-nea.org/science/wprs/shielding/sinbad/fns_duct/fnsstr-a.htm

Description:
  A large iron slab assembly (1700 x 1400 x 1800 mm) contains a doubly-bent
  (dogleg) duct with a 300 x 300 mm square cross-section. The duct has three
  legs connected at right angles: a first horizontal leg (1150 mm) along the
  beam direction, a vertical leg (600 mm) perpendicular to the beam, and a
  second horizontal leg (650 mm) parallel to but offset from the first.

  14 MeV D-T neutrons from the FNS facility enter the duct through its
  entrance on the front face of the iron assembly. The experiment studies
  neutron streaming through the duct -- how neutrons navigate the right-angle
  bends by scattering off the iron walls (albedo effect) and how the dogleg
  geometry attenuates the streaming radiation compared to a straight duct.

  This is a challenging benchmark for radiation transport codes because it
  combines streaming (void transport), deep penetration (through iron), and
  wall-scattering physics at the duct bends. Statistical convergence is
  difficult as very few source particles navigate both right-angle bends.

Geometry:
  - Iron assembly: 1700 x 1400 x 1800 mm
  - Duct cross-section: 300 x 300 mm (square)
  - 1st horizontal leg: 1150 mm along beam direction
  - Vertical leg: 600 mm perpendicular
  - 2nd horizontal leg: 650 mm parallel to first, offset vertically
  - Right-angle connections between all legs

Material:
  - Natural iron, density 7.874 g/cm3

Measurements:
  - Neutron spectra at 4 positions within and beyond the duct
  - NE213 scintillator (neutrons > 2 MeV)
  - Activation dosimetry: Nb-93(n,2n), In-115(n,n'), Au-197(n,g)

Key physics:
  - Line-of-sight streaming in the first leg
  - Wall-scattering (albedo) at both right-angle bends
  - Spectral softening through successive scatterings
  - Competition between streaming through duct and penetration through iron
