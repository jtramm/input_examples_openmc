FNG-ITER Bulk Shield Mock-up Benchmark
=======================================

Source:      SINBAD (Shielding Integral Benchmark Archive and Database)
             OECD Nuclear Energy Agency
Facility:    Frascati Neutron Generator (FNG), ENEA Frascati, Italy

URL:
  https://www.oecd-nea.org/science/wprs/shielding/sinbad/

Description:
  A ~94 cm thick mock-up simulating the ITER inboard shielding was
  irradiated by 14 MeV D-T neutrons at the FNG facility. The assembly
  reproduces the layered structure of the ITER inboard shield:

    - First wall:     Cu heat sink + SS316 back plate
    - Blanket:        SS316 structure + Perspex (water-equivalent moderator)
    - Vacuum vessel:  SS316 shells + Perspex shielding fill
    - TF coil:        Alternating Cu and SS316 plates

  The mock-up was constructed from copper, stainless steel SS316, and
  Perspex (PMMA, C5O2H8) sandwich layers. The rear section of alternating
  copper and stainless steel plates simulates the toroidal field coil
  winding pack (Nb3Sn superconductor in copper stabilizer with stainless
  steel conduit).

Geometry:
  - Total mock-up thickness: ~94 cm (17 layers)
  - Cross-section: ~60 x 60 cm
  - D-T point source at 5.3 cm from front face
  - 14 activation foil positions at depths:
    3.43, 10.32, 17.15, 23.95, 30.80, 41.85, 46.85, 53.80,
    60.55, 67.40, 74.40, 81.10, 87.75, 92.15 cm
  - 17 TLD-300 positions for nuclear heating

Materials:
  - Copper: 8.96 g/cm^3, natural isotopic composition
  - SS316:  7.93 g/cm^3 (Fe 65.4%, Cr 17%, Ni 12%, Mo 2.5%, Mn 2%, Si 1%)
  - Perspex (PMMA): 1.19 g/cm^3, C5O2H8

Measurements:
  - Activation foil reaction rates at 14 depth positions:
    Nb-93(n,2n), Al-27(n,alpha), Ni-58(n,p), Au-197(n,gamma)
  - Nuclear heating (TLD-300) at 17 positions
  - C/E ratios computed against MCNP reference calculations with
    FENDL-1 and EFF-3 nuclear data libraries

Key references:

  Frascati Neutron Generator FNG," Fusion Eng. Des., vol. 47,
  pp. 25-60, 1999.

  and Analysis of the FNG-ITER Bulk Shield Mock-up," Fusion Eng. Des.,
  2001.
