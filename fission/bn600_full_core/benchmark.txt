BN-600 Sodium-Cooled Fast Reactor Full-Core Benchmark
======================================================

Full Name:
    BN-600 Hybrid Core Neutronics Benchmark - Full Core Model

Reference:
    IAEA-TECDOC-1623, "Benchmark Analyses on the Natural Circulation Test
    Performed During the PHENIX End-of-Life Experiments," International
    Atomic Energy Agency, Vienna, 2009.

    Additional references from the IAEA fast reactor benchmark series:
    - IAEA-TECDOC-1524, "Benchmark Analyses of Sodium Natural Convection
      in the Upper Plenum of the Monju Reactor Vessel"
    - A.K. Popov et al., "BN-600 Hybrid Core Benchmark Analysis,"
      Proc. International Conference on Fast Reactors and Related Fuel
      Cycles (FR17), IAEA-CN-245, Yekaterinburg, Russia, 2017.

Facility:
    BN-600 (Beloyarsk Nuclear Power Plant, Unit 3)
    Beloyarsk, Sverdlovsk Oblast, Russia
    Operated by Rosenergoatom (Rosatom State Corporation)
    In commercial operation since April 1980

Reactor Description:
    BN-600 is a pool-type sodium-cooled fast breeder reactor producing
    1470 MWth / 600 MWe. It is one of the largest and longest-operating
    fast reactors in the world. The reactor uses a three-loop primary
    sodium circuit with intermediate heat exchangers and steam generators.

    Key design parameters:
    - Thermal power: 1470 MW
    - Electrical power: 600 MW
    - Coolant: liquid sodium
    - Core inlet temperature: ~650 K
    - Core outlet temperature: ~790 K
    - Fuel: UO2 (enriched uranium oxide)
    - Cladding: Cr16Ni15Mo3 stainless steel
    - Core height (active fuel): ~100 cm
    - Core diameter (equivalent): ~200 cm

Core Description (Hybrid Core Configuration):
    The BN-600 hybrid core uses three enrichment zones of UO2 fuel:
    - LEZ (Low Enrichment Zone): 17.0 wt% U-235, inner core
    - MEZ (Medium Enrichment Zone): 21.0 wt% U-235, middle core
    - HEZ (High Enrichment Zone): 26.0 wt% U-235, outer core

    The three-zone enrichment scheme flattens the radial power distribution,
    which is essential for achieving acceptable peak-to-average power ratios
    in fast reactor cores where radial neutron leakage is significant.

    The core is surrounded by radial blanket assemblies (depleted UO2,
    0.3 wt% U-235) and axial blanket regions (30 cm above and below
    the active fuel) for breeding.

    Each fuel assembly contains 127 wire-wrapped fuel pins in a hexagonal
    arrangement with pin pitch of 0.75 cm and assembly flat-to-flat of
    9.6 cm (assembly pitch 9.82 cm center-to-center).

Reference Results (Various codes, IAEA benchmark exercise):
    k-eff (all rods out, beginning of cycle):
    - ECCO/ERANOS:    ~1.040 - 1.050
    - MCNP:           ~1.042 - 1.058
    - SERPENT:         ~1.045 - 1.055
    - General range:   1.04 - 1.06
    - Code spread:    ~500 - 1000 pcm

    The excess reactivity is compensated by control and safety rods
    during normal operation. The operating k-eff is maintained at 1.000
    through control rod insertion.

    Differences between codes arise from:
    - Nuclear data library choice (ENDF/B, JEFF, JENDL, BROND)
    - Geometry modeling detail (heterogeneous vs. homogenized)
    - Resonance self-shielding treatment
    - Energy group structure (deterministic codes)
    - Control rod modeling (this benchmark: all rods withdrawn)

This Model:
    Full-core 3D model with explicit pin-level detail in each assembly.
    Uses openmc.HexLattice at two levels: pin lattice within assemblies
    and assembly lattice for the core. Includes axial blanket regions
    and radial blanket assemblies. Vacuum boundary conditions.

    Simplifications relative to the actual BN-600:
    - Control rod positions modeled as fuel assemblies (ARO condition)
    - No sodium plenum regions above/below blankets
    - No steel shielding assemblies
    - Uniform temperature (600 K HZP approximation)
    - No wire wrap modeled (smeared into coolant)
    - Simplified ring-based enrichment zone assignment
