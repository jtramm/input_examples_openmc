MSRE - Molten Salt Reactor Experiment
=====================================

Benchmark: IRPhEP MSRE-MSR-EXP-001
Facility: Oak Ridge National Laboratory, Oak Ridge, Tennessee, USA
Dates of Operation: 1965-1969

Description:
  The Molten Salt Reactor Experiment (MSRE) was the world's first molten-salt
  fueled reactor to reach criticality and sustained operation. A liquid fluoride
  salt (LiF-BeF2-ZrF4-UF4) served as both the fuel carrier and primary coolant.
  The salt flowed upward through channels formed between vertical graphite
  moderator stringers, exited the reactor vessel, passed through a heat
  exchanger, and was pumped back into the core. The graphite provided neutron
  moderation while the fuel salt carried the fissile material.

  The MSRE demonstrated that molten-salt reactors could operate safely and
  reliably. It proved the viability of on-line fuel processing, operated with
  both U-235 and U-233 fuel loadings, and accumulated over 13,000 equivalent
  full-power hours.
