BEAVRS Full-Core PWR Benchmark
Benchmark for Evaluation And Validation of Reactor Simulations
===============================================================================

Source Citation
---------------
N. Horelik, B. Herman, B. Forget, and K. Smith, "Benchmark for Evaluation
and Validation of Reactor Simulations (BEAVRS)," Revision 2.0.1,
MIT Computational Reactor Physics Group, Department of Nuclear Science and
Engineering, Massachusetts Institute of Technology, Cambridge, MA (2018).

Available at: https://crpg.mit.edu/research/beavrs

Overview
--------
BEAVRS is a high-fidelity, publicly available benchmark based on a real
4-loop Westinghouse pressurized water reactor (PWR). It provides detailed
as-built specifications for the first two operating cycles, including:
  - Detailed geometry specifications (pin dimensions, assembly layout)
  - Material compositions (fuel enrichments, structural materials)
  - Operating conditions (temperatures, pressures, boron concentrations)
  - Measured data (critical boron concentrations, power distributions)

The benchmark is widely used for validation of Monte Carlo and deterministic
neutron transport codes, depletion solvers, and multiphysics coupling tools.

Reactor Description
-------------------
  Type:              4-loop Westinghouse PWR
  Thermal power:     3411 MWth
  Core loading:      193 fuel assemblies (17x17 OFA design)
  Active fuel height: 365.76 cm (12 ft)
  Assembly pitch:    21.50 cm
  Pin pitch:         1.26 cm
  Fuel:              UO2 (sintered ceramic pellets)
  Cladding:          Zircaloy-4
  Coolant:           Light water with soluble boron (H3BO3)

  Cycle 1 Initial Loading:
    Region 1:  56 assemblies at 1.6 wt% U-235 (center, low leakage)
    Region 2:  64 assemblies at 2.4 wt% U-235 (middle ring)
    Region 3:  73 assemblies at 3.1 wt% U-235 (outer ring)

  Some Region 2 and Region 3 assemblies contain burnable absorber (BA)
  rods (Pyrex or WABA) for reactivity control. This simplified model
  omits BAs for the initial implementation.

Conditions Modeled (This Implementation)
-----------------------------------------
  State:             Hot Zero Power (HZP), Beginning of Cycle 1
  Control rods:      All Rods Out (ARO)
  Soluble boron:     975 ppm (measured critical boron concentration)
  Temperatures:      565 K (isothermal, all materials)
  Axial model:       2D (reflective boundaries, infinite axial extent)

  These conditions correspond to the initial criticality measurement
  performed during startup testing. The reactor is at operating temperature
  and pressure but essentially zero power (no significant temperature
  gradients or Xenon/Samarium buildup).

Reference Values
-----------------
  k-eff (measured):  0.99982 (essentially critical)

  The small deviation from exactly 1.0 is due to measurement corrections
  and the finite precision of the critical boron search.

  Typical Monte Carlo results with modern nuclear data:
    ENDF/B-VII.1:  k-eff ~ 1.001 to 1.003 (100-300 pcm positive bias)
    ENDF/B-VIII.0: k-eff ~ 1.000 to 1.002 (0-200 pcm positive bias)

  The positive bias in Monte Carlo calculations is well-documented and
  attributed to nuclear data uncertainties (primarily U-238 capture and
  inelastic scattering cross sections).

  Radial peaking factor Fxy ~ 1.2 to 1.4 for the low-leakage loading
  pattern with unrodded conditions at HZP.

Nuclear Data
------------
  This model uses ENDF/B-VIII.0 cross-section data processed into the
  HDF5 format for OpenMC.

  Data path: /data/endfb-viii.0-hdf5/cross_sections.xml

  ENDF/B-VIII.0 (Evaluated Nuclear Data File, version VIII.0) was released
  by the Cross Section Evaluation Working Group (CSEWG) in 2018. Key
  improvements over ENDF/B-VII.1 relevant to LWR analysis include:
    - Updated U-235 fission cross sections and prompt fission neutron spectra
    - Revised U-238 capture and inelastic scattering cross sections
    - Improved thermal scattering law for H in H2O
    - Updated Zr isotope evaluations

Simplifications in This Model
-------------------------------
1. No burnable absorber (BA) rods -- all guide tube positions are
   water-filled. This will cause a positive reactivity bias of several
   hundred pcm since BAs are net neutron absorbers.

2. The core baffle is approximated as a cylindrical annulus rather than
   the actual stepped rectangular structure. This slightly affects the
   water gap distribution at the core periphery.

3. The model uses a 2D axial approximation (reflective top/bottom).
   This eliminates axial leakage and avoids modeling the upper/lower
   core plates, nozzles, and water reflector regions. For HZP conditions
   with unrodded fuel, the axial power distribution is nearly symmetric
   and this is a reasonable approximation for radial power studies.

4. All materials are at a uniform temperature of 565 K. At HZP there
   are no significant temperature gradients, making this an excellent
   approximation for the isothermal critical condition.

5. Fresh fuel (no burnup or fission product inventory). This is correct
   for the Beginning of Cycle 1 condition.

Assembly Layout (17x17 pin positions)
--------------------------------------
Each assembly has 289 positions in a 17x17 square lattice:
  - 264 fuel rod positions (UO2 pellets in Zircaloy-4 cladding)
  - 24 guide tube positions (water-filled Zircaloy-4 tubes)
  - 1 instrument tube position (center, position 8,8)

Guide tube positions (0-indexed row, column):
  (2,5)  (2,8)  (2,11)
  (3,3)  (3,13)
  (5,2)  (5,5)  (5,8)  (5,11)  (5,14)
  (8,2)  (8,5)         (8,11)  (8,14)
  (11,2) (11,5) (11,8) (11,11) (11,14)
  (13,3) (13,13)
  (14,5) (14,8) (14,11)

Instrument tube position: (8,8) -- center of the assembly

Running the Model
------------------
  1. Build and export XML files:
       python model.py --export

  2. Run the simulation:
       python model.py --run

  3. Run with custom settings:
       python model.py --run --particles 50000 --batches 500 --inactive 100

  4. Generate geometry plots:
       python visualize.py

  5. Analyze results:
       python analysis.py

  Default settings: 20,000 particles/batch, 200 batches, 50 inactive.
  This gives 150 active batches x 20,000 = 3,000,000 active histories,
  which provides k-eff precision of approximately +/- 10-20 pcm and
  assembly power uncertainties of 1-3%.

  For publication-quality results, use 50,000+ particles and 500+ batches.

Computational Requirements
---------------------------
  A full-core BEAVRS calculation with 193 assemblies and 17x17 pins per
  assembly involves ~55,000 unique fuel pin cells. With the default
  settings (3M active histories), expect:
    - Runtime: 30-60 minutes on a modern workstation (serial)
    - Memory: ~2-4 GB
    - Disk: ~100-500 MB for statepoint files

  OpenMC supports shared-memory parallelism (OpenMP threads) for
  significant speedup on multi-core systems.
