McSAFER NuScale-like SMR Neutronics Benchmark
==============================================

Paper:    "Neutronics Benchmark of the NuScale-like SMR in the McSAFER Project"
Authors:  J. Bousquet et al.
Journal:  Journal of Nuclear Engineering, 2025, 6(4), 44
URL:      https://www.mdpi.com/2673-4362/6/4/44

Project:  McSAFER (Multi-Criteria Analysis of Safety, Environmental, and
          Financial aspects of Energy systems based on Renewable sources
          and small modular Reactors)
Funding:  EU Horizon 2020 (Grant Agreement No. 945063)

Description
-----------
This benchmark defines a NuScale-like Small Modular Reactor (SMR) core for
code-to-code comparison of neutronics calculations. The NuScale Power Module
is a 77 MWe (250 MWth) integral pressurized water reactor designed for
factory fabrication and modular deployment, with up to 12 modules per plant.

Key Design Features
-------------------
- Compact core: 37 fuel assemblies (17x17 Westinghouse-type)
- Short active fuel length: 200 cm (vs ~366 cm in a standard PWR)
- Natural circulation primary cooling (no reactor coolant pumps)
- BORON-FREE OPERATION: no dissolved boric acid in the moderator
  (reactivity control via Gd2O3 burnable absorbers and control rods)
- Seven assembly types varying in enrichment (2.0%, 2.6%, 3.1% U-235)
  and gadolinium content (0, 4, 8, 12, 16 Gd pins per assembly)

This Model (Implemented Here)
------------------------------
- Single assembly: 3.1 wt% U-235 enriched UO2, no gadolinium
  (the simplest assembly type in the benchmark)
- 2D infinite lattice with reflective boundary conditions
- Hot full power conditions: 900K fuel, 557K moderator
- All control rods withdrawn (ARO)
- Zero soluble boron in moderator

Reference Solution
------------------
Code:     Serpent 2 (continuous-energy Monte Carlo)
Library:  ENDF/B-VII.1 or JEFF-3.3 (varies by participant)
k-eff:    ~1.18 (approximate, for the 3.1% assembly without Gd, fresh fuel)

Multiple participants calculated this benchmark using various codes:
  - Serpent 2 (VTT, reference)
  - OpenMC
  - MCNP
  - SCALE/KENO
  - MORET
  - Various deterministic codes

Reported code-to-code differences are on the order of 200-500 pcm,
with the OpenMC vs Serpent difference approximately 355 pcm.
