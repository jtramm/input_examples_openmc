BEAVRS - Benchmark for Evaluation And Validation of Reactor Simulations
========================================================================

Document: BEAVRS v1.0.1 Specification
Authors:  N. Horelik, B. Herman, B. Forget, K. Smith
Org:      MIT Computational Reactor Physics Group
URL:      https://github.com/mit-crpg/BEAVRS

Description
-----------
BEAVRS is a benchmark based on REAL MEASURED DATA from an operating
Westinghouse 4-loop pressurized water reactor rated at 3411 MWth. It
provides detailed specifications of the reactor geometry, materials,
and operating conditions, along with actual plant measurements for
validation of reactor physics simulation codes.

Unlike many computational benchmarks that compare code-to-code results,
BEAVRS enables true validation against reality. The measured data includes:
  - Hot zero power (HZP) critical boron concentrations
  - Boron letdown curves for Cycle 1 and Cycle 2
  - Three-dimensional in-core flux maps from 58 instrumented assemblies
  - Control rod bank worths
  - Isothermal temperature coefficients
  - Startup physics test results

Hot Zero Power Conditions
--------------------------
  Fuel temperature:       600 K
  Moderator temperature:  565 K
  Moderator density:      0.740 g/cc
  System pressure:        155.13 bar (2250 psia)
  Soluble boron:          ~975 ppm (measured critical concentration)

Reference Measured Data
------------------------
HZP critical boron concentration: ~975 ppm

This is the soluble boron concentration at which the full core
(193 assemblies with mixed enrichment loading, all control rods
withdrawn) achieves criticality at hot zero power conditions.

Important: This is a FULL CORE measurement. A single-assembly
infinite-lattice calculation at 975 ppm boron will give k-eff > 1.0
because:
  1. No radial neutron leakage (reflective BCs)
  2. No axial neutron leakage (2D model)
  3. Uniform enrichment (real core has mixed loading)
  4. No burnable absorbers (some assemblies have BPs in the real core)

Comparison with VERA Benchmark
-------------------------------
BEAVRS and VERA are complementary benchmarks:

BEAVRS:
  - Based on real plant, real measured data
  - True validation (code vs. reality)
  - More complex (full plant specification)
  - Critical boron is the primary measured quantity

VERA:
  - Computational benchmark (code vs. code)
  - Reference is a converged Monte Carlo solution
  - Simpler, progression of problems
  - k-eff is the primary comparison quantity
  - Based on Watts Bar Unit 1 (also a Westinghouse 4-loop PWR)

Both use the standard Westinghouse 17x17 fuel assembly design, but
with slightly different dimensions reflecting different plant vintages.
