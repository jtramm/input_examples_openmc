HTR-10 Pebble Bed High-Temperature Gas-Cooled Reactor
======================================================

Facility
--------
The HTR-10 is a 10 MWth pebble bed high-temperature gas-cooled reactor
(HTGR) built and operated by the Institute of Nuclear and New Energy
Technology (INET) at Tsinghua University, Beijing, China. It achieved
first criticality on December 1, 2000, and full power operation in
January 2003.

The HTR-10 was designed as a test reactor to demonstrate the inherent
safety features of modular pebble bed HTGR technology, including:
  - Passive decay heat removal without active cooling systems
  - Negative temperature coefficient preventing power excursions
  - Ability to withstand loss of coolant without fuel damage

Benchmark Description
---------------------
The HTR-10 initial criticality benchmark involves determining the critical
loading height of fuel pebbles in the cylindrical core cavity. The core
was loaded incrementally with fuel pebbles (interspersed with graphite
moderator pebbles in some loading configurations) until criticality was
achieved.

Key specifications:
  - Core cavity:     180 cm diameter x ~500 cm height (conical bottom)
  - Fuel pebbles:    6.0 cm diameter (3.0 cm radius)
  - Fuel zone:       5.0 cm diameter (2.5 cm radius) within each pebble
  - TRISO particles: 8,335 per fuel pebble
  - UO2 enrichment:  17 wt% U-235
  - Coolant:         Helium at ~3 MPa
  - Reflector:       Nuclear-grade graphite

Critical height measurement: approximately 123 cm (16,890 fuel pebbles)

Reference Calculations
----------------------
Multiple codes have been used to analyze the HTR-10 initial criticality:

  Code        k-eff           Notes
  ----        -----           -----
  MCNP        1.027+/-0.002   Homogenized pebble model, ~126 cm height
  VSOP        1.036           Diffusion theory with cell homogenization
  Serpent     ~1.01-1.03      Varies with modeling detail
  SCALE       ~1.02-1.04      KENO-VI Monte Carlo

These calculations generally predict k-eff slightly above 1.0 at the
measured critical height, with the spread arising from differences in:
  - Cross section libraries and processing
  - Treatment of double heterogeneity (explicit vs. homogenized TRISO)
  - Geometric simplifications (cylindrical vs. actual cone-bottom core)
  - Temperature distributions

The Double Heterogeneity Challenge
-----------------------------------
TRISO-fueled pebble bed reactors exhibit "double heterogeneity":

  1st level: TRISO micro-particles randomly dispersed in graphite matrix
     - Self-shielding of UO2 kernels affects resonance absorption
     - Explicit modeling vs. homogenization can cause ~500-2000 pcm bias

  2nd level: Fuel pebbles randomly packed in the core cavity
     - Inter-pebble streaming of neutrons
     - Spatial self-shielding at the pebble scale

Correctly capturing both levels of heterogeneity is a key challenge for
reactor physics codes analyzing pebble bed reactors.
