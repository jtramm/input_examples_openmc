VVER-1000 MOX Core Computational Benchmark
=============================================

Full Name:
    VVER-1000 MOX Core Computational Benchmark

Reference:
    V. Boyarinov, G. Lazarenko, Yu. Semionov, et al.,
    "VVER-1000 MOX Core Computational Benchmark,"
    NEA/NSC/DOC(2005)17, OECD Nuclear Energy Agency, 2005.

    ISBN: 92-64-01081-X

Organization:
    Expert Group on Reactor-based Plutonium Disposition,
    Nuclear Science Committee, OECD Nuclear Energy Agency (NEA)

Benchmark Description:
    This benchmark evaluates the capability of reactor physics codes to
    analyze a VVER-1000 core loaded with weapons-grade MOX fuel. The core
    contains 163 hexagonal fuel assemblies arranged in a hexagonal lattice,
    with approximately 30% of assemblies containing MOX fuel and the
    remainder containing UOX fuel of various enrichments and burnup levels.

    The weapons-grade plutonium isotopic vector is:
      Pu-239: 93.6 at%
      Pu-240: 5.9 at%
      Pu-241: 0.4 at%
      Pu-242: 0.1 at%

    MOX assemblies use three radial zones with different PuO2 content
    (2.4/2.7/3.6 wt% PuO2) to flatten the assembly power distribution.

    The benchmark specifies multiple reactor states (S1-S6) with different
    operating conditions. State S1 (HZP, ARO) is the primary eigenvalue
    benchmark:
      - Hot zero power: 552 K uniform temperature
      - All rods out (ARO)
      - 600 ppm soluble boron
      - Fresh fuel (beginning of cycle)

Core Design:
    Reactor type:       VVER-1000 (hexagonal PWR)
    Thermal power:      3000 MWth
    Core assemblies:    163 hexagonal
    Assembly pitch:     23.6 cm (flat-to-flat)
    Pin pitch:          1.275 cm (triangular lattice)
    Pins per assembly:  312 fuel + 18 guide tubes + 1 instrument tube = 331
    Fuel pellet radius: 0.386 cm
    Clad outer radius:  0.458 cm
    Active fuel height: 353 cm
    Core diameter:      ~316 cm (equivalent)

Reference Results (State S1, from benchmark participants):
    MCU Monte Carlo:    keff ~ 1.000-1.010
    TVS-M:              keff ~ 1.005
    HELIOS/BIPR:        keff ~ 1.003
    WIMS/RFSP:          keff ~ 1.004
    APOLLO/CRONOS:      keff ~ 1.006

    Spread among participants: ~500 pcm
    The benchmark is designed as a near-critical configuration.

Additional Benchmark States:
    S2: HFP (hot full power, non-uniform temperature distribution)
    S3-S4: Control rod worth measurements
    S5-S6: Isothermal temperature coefficient measurements

This Model:
    Full-core 2D model with 163 assemblies, ~30% MOX loading.
    Uses simplified material compositions (burned fuel approximated as
    fresh UOX with reduced enrichment). State S1 conditions.
    Pin-resolved geometry with explicit fuel, gap, clad, and moderator
    regions. Radial reflector includes steel baffle and water.
