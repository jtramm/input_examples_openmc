DESCRIPTION
-----------
The SNAP-10A (Systems for Nuclear Auxiliary Power) was a compact nuclear
fission reactor developed by Atomics International (AI) in the 1960s for
space applications. It was the first and only nuclear fission reactor to
operate in space, launched aboard the SNAPSHOT satellite on April 3, 1965,
producing 500 watts of electrical power via thermoelectric converters.

The SCA-4B (SNAP Critical Assembly 4B) experimental program evaluated the
criticality safety of SNAP 10A/2 reactor cores under water immersion
scenarios. The experiments were performed in multiple phases at the AI
facility, with configurations varying the number of fuel elements, water
reflection, internal beryllium reflectors, and absorber sleeves.

This benchmark model implements Case 8490a from Phase I of the SCA-4B
program, which is the simplest experimental configuration:
  - 28 SCA-4 fuel elements in the first 28 lattice positions (of 37 total)
  - No Lucite rods in vacant positions (filled with water)
  - No internal beryllium reflector inserts
  - Core vessel completely flooded with water
  - Full water reflection (9 inches in upper tank, lower tank full)
  - Room temperature (~293.6 K)

The assembly was subcritical by approximately 0.02 dollars.

REACTOR CORE DESCRIPTION
------------------------
The reactor core consists of 37 fuel element positions arranged on a
triangular pitch of 1.260 inches (3.2004 cm) inside a cylindrical 316
stainless steel vessel with an inner diameter of 8.900 inches (22.606 cm).

Each fuel element is a solid cylindrical rod of U-ZrH (uranium-zirconium
hydride) fuel clad in Hastelloy N (a nickel-molybdenum alloy):
  - Fuel rod diameter: 1.212 inches (3.07848 cm)
  - Cladding tube OD: 1.250 inches (3.175 cm), wall thickness 0.010 in.
  - Fuel rod length: 12.225 inches (31.0515 cm)
  - Total element length: 12.450 inches (31.623 cm) including end caps
  - Fuel composition: ~10 wt% U (>= 93 wt% U-235) + ~90 wt% ZrH
  - Fuel density: 6.036 g/cm3
  - Each element contains approximately 128.5 g U-235

The gap between fuel rod and cladding is filled with a hydrogen barrier
coating containing samarium oxide (Sm2O3) as a burnable neutron absorber.

Water tanks surround the vessel for reflection:
  - Upper tank: provides radial reflection over upper 1/3 of vessel plus
    axial reflection above (6 inches radial water gap)
  - Lower tank: provides radial reflection over lower 2/3 of vessel
    (6.5 inches radial water gap)
  - Control cap tank: small tank below for axial reflection from below

NOTES
-----
- All material number densities are taken directly from Appendix B of
  ORNL/TM-2005/54 to ensure consistency with the MCNP5 reference.
- Geometry dimensions are from Appendix C of the same report.
- The thermal scattering treatment for H in ZrH and Zr in ZrH is applied
  to the fuel material, and H in H2O is applied to water.

  thermal-spectrum reactor (~73% of fissions from thermal neutrons).
