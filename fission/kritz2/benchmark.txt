KRITZ-2 Critical Experiments Benchmark
=======================================

Source:  OECD/NEA Benchmark on the KRITZ-2 LEU and MOX Critical Experiments
Report: NEA/NSC/DOC(2005)24
URL:    https://www.oecd-nea.org/upload/docs/application/pdf/2019-12/nsc-doc2005-24.pdf

Description:
  The KRITZ reactor operated at Studsvik, Sweden, during the first half of
  the 1970s. The "KRITZ experiments" included a series of light-water-moderated
  lattices with uranium rods, mixed-oxide rods, or both, at room temperature
  and elevated temperatures up to ~245 deg C. Criticality was attained by
  controlling the boron content in the water and by adjusting the water level.

  Three KRITZ-2 configurations are included in the benchmark:
    - KRITZ-2:1  -- UO2, 1.86 wt% U-235, 14.85 mm pitch, 44x44 rods
    - KRITZ-2:13 -- UO2, 1.86 wt% U-235, 16.35 mm pitch, 40x40 rods
    - KRITZ-2:19 -- MOX (weapons-grade Pu), 18.00 mm pitch, 25x24 rods

  Each configuration was measured at room temperature (~20 deg C) and at
  elevated temperature (~245 deg C). Criticality was achieved by adjusting
  the boron concentration in the moderator water and the water level.

Reference k-eff values (average of 13 participant solutions):
  KRITZ-2:1  cold (19.7 deg C):  0.99463 +/- 0.00330
  KRITZ-2:1  hot  (248.5 deg C): 0.99290 +/- 0.00346
  KRITZ-2:13 cold (22.1 deg C):  0.99736 +/- 0.00204
  KRITZ-2:13 hot  (243.0 deg C): 0.99605 +/- 0.00280
  KRITZ-2:19 cold (21.1 deg C):  0.99908 +/- (not given)
  KRITZ-2:19 hot  (235.9 deg C): 0.99822 +/- (not given)

  Note: The experimental value should be 1.0 (critical). The average
  predictions below 1.0 indicate a systematic under-prediction by most
  codes, likely due to nuclear data and/or thermal scattering limitations.

ICSBEP identifiers:
  LEU-COMP-THERM-001 (KRITZ-2:1 and KRITZ-2:13)
  MIX-COMP-THERM-001 (KRITZ-2:19)

This benchmark is particularly valuable for:
  - Validating temperature-dependent cross sections
  - Testing thermal scattering (S(alpha,beta)) treatments
  - Assessing Doppler broadening implementations
  - Comparing UO2 vs MOX prediction accuracy
