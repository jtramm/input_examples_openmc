IAEA 10 MW MTR Research Reactor Benchmark
==========================================

Source Document
---------------
IAEA-TECDOC-643, "Research Reactor Core Conversion Guidebook"
Volume 1: Summary
International Atomic Energy Agency, Vienna, 1992

URL: https://www-pub.iaea.org/MTCD/Publications/PDF/te_643v1_prn.pdf

Description
-----------
The IAEA 10 MW MTR (Material Testing Reactor) benchmark is one of the most
widely reproduced research reactor benchmarks in the world. It was established
as part of the Reduced Enrichment for Research and Test Reactors (RERTR) program
to provide a standardized calculational benchmark for comparing neutronic methods
used in research reactor analysis.

The benchmark specifies an idealized pool-type 10 MW research reactor with
plate-type fuel elements containing highly enriched uranium (HEU, 93 wt% U-235)
in a uranium-aluminum alloy fuel meat clad with aluminum. The core consists of
23 standard fuel elements (SFE) and 5 control fuel elements (CFE) arranged in a
5x6 rectangular grid, surrounded by beryllium and graphite reflectors.

Key specifications:
- Thermal power: 10 MW
- Fuel type: UAl alloy (uranium-aluminum), plate geometry
- Enrichment: 93 wt% U-235 (HEU reference case)
- U-235 loading: 280 g per standard fuel element
- 23 fuel plates per standard fuel element
- Active fuel height: 60 cm
- Reflector: Beryllium (inner) + Graphite (outer)

Reference results from multiple international participants are provided in the
TECDOC, including k-eff, power distributions, and control rod worths.

Published reference k-eff values for the fresh HEU core range from approximately
1.05 to 1.08, depending on the code and nuclear data library used.
