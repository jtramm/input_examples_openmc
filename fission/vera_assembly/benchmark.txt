Description
-----------
This document defines a progression of benchmark problems for the
Virtual Environment for Reactor Applications (VERA), developed by
the Consortium for Advanced Simulation of Light Water Reactors
(CASL). CASL is a U.S. Department of Energy Innovation Hub focused
on advanced modeling and simulation of commercial nuclear reactors.

The benchmarks are based on the Watts Bar Nuclear Unit 1 reactor,
a Westinghouse 4-loop PWR. They start from simple configurations
and progress in complexity:
  - Problem 1: Single pin cells (A through E, varying conditions)
  - Problem 2: Lattice problems (2x2 and full assemblies)
  - Problem 3: Single fuel assemblies with control rods
  - Problem 4: Multi-assembly (color-set) problems
  - Problem 5: Full core models

Problem 2A (implemented here) is a full 17x17 PWR fuel assembly
at hot zero power with all control rods withdrawn. It contains
264 fuel pins, 24 guide tubes, and 1 instrument tube arranged in
the standard Westinghouse layout. Reflective boundary conditions
simulate an infinite lattice of identical assemblies.

Reference Solution for Problem 2A
-----------------------------------
Code:       CE KENO-VI (continuous energy Monte Carlo)
Library:    ENDF/B-VII.0
k-eff:      1.18703 +/- 0.00009
Particles:  High statistics (converged reference)

The reference was computed using the SCALE/KENO-VI Monte Carlo code
with continuous-energy cross sections from ENDF/B-VII.0.
